# Design System: Team Building 2026
**Project ID:** teambuilding-os-13

## 1. Visual Theme & Atmosphere
This landing page should feel like an internal company festival seen through a premium event-brand lens: high-energy, celebratory, youthful, and polished rather than childish. The mood lives between "startup launch page" and "team arena" with a dark midnight base, glowing coral and gold highlights, plus cool teal and electric blue accents that keep the page feeling fast, fresh, and social.

The visual rhythm should alternate between:
- playful motion and rounded geometry for the fun, laughter-first moments
- sharp contrast, split layouts, and scoreboard metaphors for the competitive moments
- focused, lower-light surfaces for the strategy and mind-game moments

Overall density should be medium. Give the page generous breathing room, but punctuate it with bold blocks, oversized headlines, floating decorative forms, and softly glowing cards so the interface feels alive.

## 2. Color Palette & Roles
- **Midnight Event Canvas** (`#041422`): Primary page background and the anchoring surface behind every section.
- **Deep Ocean Layer** (`#071a2e`): Secondary dark gradient tone used to create depth across large page areas.
- **Festival White** (`#f8fbff`): Primary text and high-contrast content color.
- **Sunset Coral** (`#ff8c61`): Used for playful energy, food-game storytelling, warm glows, and lively highlights.
- **Victory Gold** (`#ffcb57`): Used for hero emphasis, premium CTA gradients, and "winning moment" accents.
- **Strategy Teal** (`#35d6c4`): Used for logic-heavy states, subtle technical energy, and analytical game visuals.
- **Arena Blue** (`#5f7cff`): Used for battle framing, scoreboard emphasis, and versus-driven interactions.
- **Mist Border** (`rgba(255,255,255,0.12)`): Used for glassmorphism outlines, quiet dividers, and soft structural edges.
- **Dark Glass Surface** (`rgba(8,24,39,0.78)`): Used for floating cards, sticky navigation, and elevated content panels.

## 3. Typography Rules
- **Display font:** `Syne`
  Use for hero titles, section headings, numbers with impact, and short punchy copy. It should feel athletic, expressive, and current.
- **Body font:** `Manrope`
  Use for descriptions, supporting copy, small labels, and UI text. It should remain easy to scan on both mobile and desktop.

Typography should follow these behaviors:
- Headlines are tight, large, and slightly compressed with strong tracking control.
- Supporting text is airy, readable, and never visually dense.
- Eyebrows and metadata use uppercase spacing to create an "event system" feel.
- Buttons and badges should read confidently, with medium-to-bold weight and short copy.

## 4. Component Stylings
* **Buttons:** Rounded full-pill buttons with either a warm gold-to-coral gradient for primary CTAs or frosted dark glass for secondary actions. Hover should feel like a subtle lift, not a jump.
* **Cards and Panels:** Large rounded corners with translucent dark surfaces, soft border strokes, and atmospheric blur. Each major game card gets its own colored glow derived from its narrative identity.
* **Badges:** Compact rounded chips with uppercase tracking; used for headcount, game mood, and metadata.
* **Navigation:** Sticky, slim, and frosted. It should stay out of the way while still signaling polish.
* **Decorative Objects:** Floating circles, rounded blocks, glows, and mesh-like gradients should reinforce movement and festivity without cluttering reading flow.

## 5. Layout Principles
The layout should feel cinematic rather than corporate-grid rigid:
- Use a wide central container with generous horizontal padding.
- Hero should open with a strong split composition: large copy on one side, live event preview on the other.
- Overview cards should scan quickly in a 3-column rhythm on desktop and stack cleanly on mobile.
- Detailed game sections should feel like "mini stages" with alternating content-visual balance.
- Space between sections should be deliberate and generous so each game keeps its own identity.

## 6. Motion & Interaction
- Motion should be smooth, layered, and purposeful.
- Hero text enters with fade + slide-up sequencing.
- Overview cards and detail panels reveal on scroll with staggered timing.
- CTA buttons use subtle hover lift and tap compression.
- Floating shapes and highlight chips should drift slowly to keep the page alive.
- Reduced motion must be respected by collapsing decorative animation to static states.

## 7. Narrative Mapping
- **Món Ăn Bingo:** Warm coral and gold, pop/bounce behavior, playful card-grid metaphors, "cute chaos" energy.
- **Hoàng Đế Đại Chiến:** Blue vs coral split composition, scoreboard language, draft and arena motifs, tactical intensity.
- **Chiến Lược Gia Tài Ba:** Teal and low-light surfaces, asymmetric information visuals, betting progress, focused tension.

## 8. Do's and Don'ts
### Do
- Keep the page feeling energetic but still premium.
- Use one clear primary CTA per zone.
- Let each game have a distinct visual personality while staying inside one system.
- Maintain strong contrast and touch-friendly interactions.

### Don't
- Don't use emoji as structural icons.
- Don't overload every section with equal animation intensity.
- Don't flatten the page into generic SaaS blocks.
- Don't use pastel-on-white or purple-first gradients; the identity should feel warmer, bolder, and more event-specific.
